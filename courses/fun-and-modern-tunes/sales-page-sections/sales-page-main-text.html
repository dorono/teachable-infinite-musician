By Danie Skree

Use the Excitement that Brought You to the Sax to Sound Great Sooner and Stay Motivated.

Whatever music it was that first sparked you or your child’s interest in the sax, chances are that it wasn’t simplistic nursery rhymes or boring exercises.

Unfortunately, these types of tunes are precisely what you find in the vast majority of beginner saxophone practice books.

While it may seem that these types of songs are necessary for learning the basics of the instrument, they’re actually slowing down you or your child’s progress.

After decades spent taking students of all ages from beginners to outstanding players, I can tell you one thing for sure…

Playing music that excites and inspires you is the faster way to build foundational skills.

Striking a Balance Between Fundamentals and Fun

At the end of the day, the primary goal of a beginning saxophonist practice book is to get you:

  • Fluent with the fingerings for all the notes on the instrument
  • Reading music
  • Strengthening the embouchure
  • Improving articulation and use of dynamics
  • Developing understanding of rhythm
  • Using techniques such as trills and tremolos
  • Learning basic music theory

But none of that is going to happen if practicing starts feeling like a chore.

And likewise, the discouragement that comes from attempting to tackle music that’s not suited towards beginners is also going to stop progress dead in its tracks.

A New Pathway to Sounding Great Sooner and Staying Motivated

After seeing that the books typically found at the top of a search on Amazon.com were driving many students into that proverbial wall of de-motivation, I developed my own program of learning material that would:

  • Teach ALL of the fundamentals found in the top-selling beginner sax books
  • Provide unmistakably superior outcomes by harnessing the passion and excitement that first drew the student to the instrument

Based on the incredible success of this approach, I created Fun and Modern Tunes for Beginner Saxophonists.

Far more than just a practice book, this program is:

  • An ebook of 25 songs, with each song written in a modern style of music that’s rarely, if ever, included in a saxophone method book for beginners.
  • An accompanying set of backing tracks for each song, with each track transposed to make it easy to play along - regardless of whether you play alto, tenor, baritone, or soprano sax
  • Demonstration tracks for each song with me playing each tune over the backing track to eliminate any guesswork when it comes to knowing exactly what the “end result” is supposed to sound like.
